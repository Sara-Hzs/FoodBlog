Pistachio Pesto

Welcome to our delicious Pistachio Pesto Pasta recipe! This dish is perfect for those who crave a comforting yet easy-to-make meal. Whether you're cooking for your family, friends, or just yourself, this recipe is a great go-to option.

Our Sicilian-inspired recipe puts a unique twist on the classic basil pesto by using pistachios instead of pine nuts. The result is a fresh, zesty pesto bursting with flavor. Plus, pistachios are a great addition to any diet as they're lower in calories than many other nuts and packed with protein, fiber, and antioxidants.

Making this pesto is a breeze. Simply blend all the ingredients in a food processor or blender until smooth. For those who prefer a chunkier consistency, use the pulse setting on your food processor.

While your pasta cooks, you can easily whip up this pesto. Once the pasta is cooked, simply toss it with the pesto in the warm pot, using reserved pasta water to create a delicious, creamy sauce that clings to the pasta. We love using orecchiette pasta with this pistachio pesto, but you can use any pasta shape you prefer.

Not only is this dish great with pasta, but it's also versatile enough to be used in many other recipes. Try it mixed with yogurt or a cream cheese like ricotta or mascarpone for a tasty dip. Or simply spread it on some Italian bread or a toasted panini with some creamy mozzarella and sliced tomato.

We hope you enjoy making and eating this Pistachio Pesto Pasta recipe as much as we do!


TOOLS:

  • Blender or food processor

INGREDIENTS:

  • 150 grams of pistachio kernels
  • 10 grams of fresh basil
  • 75 grams of grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 120 ml of olive oil
  • Â½ tsp of salt
  • Zest from 1 lemon

Optional Garnishes:

  • Roughly chopped pistachio kernels
  • Grated parmesan cheese

To prepare a mouth-watering pasta dish, follow these simple instructions:

  1. Grab a large pot and cook your preferred pasta shapes in salted water, according to the instructions on the packet. Before draining the pasta, make sure to reserve one cup of the cooking water.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, whip up the pistachio pesto. Take fresh basil, parmesan cheese, garlic, olive oil, salt, lemon zest, and some roughly chopped pistachio kernels, and blend them all together in a food processor or blender until the mixture is smooth. For an extra zing, feel free to add more salt or lemon zest to suit your taste.
  3. Add the freshly made pesto to the warm pot that was used to cook the pasta. Mix it well but avoid heating it up. Add the cooked pasta and stir thoroughly.
  4. Gradually pour in the reserved pasta cooking water, a little at a time, until you reach the desired sauciness level. The cooking water will help the pesto stick to the pasta, ensuring every bite is packed with flavor.
  5. To elevate your dish, garnish it with grated parmesan cheese and/or some chopped pistachios.
  6. This dish can serve up to six people, so you can always store leftovers in the fridge for up to five days or in the freezer for future indulgence. Enjoy!
